Where treaty 3 was negotiated

Treaty 3 was negotiated in Fort Frances, Fort William and the final negotiations of treaty was on October 3rd, 1873 in North-West angle.

What's Unique about Treaty 3?

Treaty 3 differs from the other treaty's due to it's half-breed adhesion. Its the only of the numbered treaties to ignore the typical exclusion of Metis from treaties. In 1874 the metis surrounding rainy lake and rainy river requested to join the treaty. They were included in an adhesion in 1875 and promised the same benefits as the indigenous in the treaty. Canada then created a policy where metis had to become "indians" in order to benefit from treaty #3, completely nullifying the effect of the half-breed adhesion.

Treaty 3 today

Grand Council Treaty #3 is an administrative body that speaks for the 28 First nations and 25,000 indigenous people, their reach extends over 55,000 square miles of land. Treaty 3 land even got its own police force in 2003 called the TTPS (treaty three police service). Francis Kavanaugh is the current elected chief and is currently serving his third term.
